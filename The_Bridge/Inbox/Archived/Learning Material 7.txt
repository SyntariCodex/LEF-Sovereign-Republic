### **Source 1: Financial Physics**

**Title:** Impact of Order Book Asymmetries on Cryptocurrency Prices
**Context:** An econometric study analyzing how the "shape" of market depth predicts price movements, specifically in Bitcoin markets.

**Core Contribution:**
Price changes are not just driven by trade volume (buying/selling) but by the *structure* of liquidity sitting on the books. The paper introduces **Order Book Asymmetry (OBA)** as a predictive metric. If the "slope" of liquidity on the Bid side is steeper than the Ask side, prices tend to rise, and vice versa.

**Key Concepts:**

* **Limit Order Book (LOB):** The list of all pending buy and sell orders.
* **Market Depth:** The cumulative volume available at different price levels.
* **The Slope Metric:** Instead of just counting volume, calculate the *elasticity* (slope) of the depth. High slope = strong resistance to price movement.
* **Asymmetry:** The imbalance between the Bid Slope and Ask Slope is a strong short-term predictor of price velocity.

### **1. Order Book Asymmetry (OBA) Calculation**

**Goal:** Predict short-term price direction by measuring the "slope" of liquidity depth.

```python
def calculate_order_book_slope(order_book, side, depth_level=10):
    # side: "BID" or "ASK"
    # depth_level: How many price levels deep to check
    
    cumulative_volume = 0
    price_delta = 0
    best_price = order_book[side][0].price
    
    for i in range(depth_level):
        order = order_book[side][i]
        cumulative_volume += order.volume
        price_delta = abs(order.price - best_price)
        
    # Slope = Change in Volume / Change in Price
    # High Slope = "Wall" of liquidity (Hard to move price)
    slope = cumulative_volume / price_delta
    return slope

def predict_price_movement(order_book):
    bid_slope = calculate_order_book_slope(order_book, "BID")
    ask_slope = calculate_order_book_slope(order_book, "ASK")
    
    # OBA: Normalized Asymmetry Metric
    # Range: -1 to +1
    oba = (bid_slope - ask_slope) / (bid_slope + ask_slope)
    
    if oba > 0.2:
        return "BULLISH_SIGNAL" # Bids are steeper (stronger support)
    elif oba < -0.2:
        return "BEARISH_SIGNAL" # Asks are steeper (stronger resistance)
    else:
        return "NEUTRAL"

```
