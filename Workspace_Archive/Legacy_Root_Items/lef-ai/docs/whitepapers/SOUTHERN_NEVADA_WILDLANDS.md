III. The Operational Model: The Synapse
SNW functions as a decentralized governance protocol. It detects needs and signals connections via funding and frameworks, enabling collaborations without direct operational control.

The Workflow:
	•	Request for Resilience (RFR): SNW releases quarterly objectives based on seasonal or community needs (e.g., "Q2 Objective: Enhance land navigation skills for 50 youth in Henderson via equestrian or outdoor challenges").

	•	The Bid: Nodes (Partners) submit "Action Plans" to leverage SNW's network.
	•	Example: A youth organization proposes partnering with a horse ranch for weekly sessions, providing supervision while the ranch handles expertise.

	•	The Grant: SNW awards funding to cover costs (e.g., venue fees, transport).

	•	The Execution: Partners handle staffing and supervision. SNW supplies advisory "Standards of Operations"—high-level best practices, not mandatory directives.

Why This Works:
	•	Liability Management: Liability remains with the providers (partners and venues). SNW's role as donor/sponsor avoids vicarious liability.

	•	Scale: Fund multiple programs simultaneously without internal staffing, allowing expansion to new collaborations.

	•	Regulatory Safety: Fiat grants reduce AML/KYC complexities initially. If issues arise, SNW's indirect role and partner vetting provide strong defenses.
